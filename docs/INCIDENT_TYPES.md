# Incident Types Documentation

## Overview
This document outlines the various types of incidents that can occur within supported industries including public sector, medical, manufacturing, logistics, insurance, retail, utilities, construction, corporate, technology, and education. Each section includes specific incident examples and classifications to aid in the understanding and management of these incidents.

## Public Sector
- **Incident Type**: Natural Disaster
  - **Example**: Flooding, Earthquake
  - **Classification**: Emergency, Response Required

- **Incident Type**: Cyber Attack
  - **Example**: Hack on government databases
  - **Classification**: Security Breach, Investigation Required

## Medical
- **Incident Type**: Medication Error
  - **Example**: Wrong dosage given to a patient
  - **Classification**: Patient Safety, Review Required

- **Incident Type**: Equipment Failure
  - **Example**: MRI machine breakdown
  - **Classification**: Operational, Maintenance Required

## Manufacturing
- **Incident Type**: Workplace Injury
  - **Example**: Employee injured by machinery
  - **Classification**: Safety Incident, Report Required

- **Incident Type**: Product Recall
  - **Example**: Defective automobile part
  - **Classification**: Quality Control, Customer Notification Required

## Logistics
- **Incident Type**: Delivery Delay
  - **Example**: Truck breakdown
  - **Classification**: Supply Chain, Communication Required

- **Incident Type**: Product Damage
  - **Example**: Forklift accident in the warehouse
  - **Classification**: Inventory Loss, Report Required

## Insurance
- **Incident Type**: Fraudulent Claim
  - **Example**: Staged automobile accident
  - **Classification**: Investigation, Legal Action Possible

- **Incident Type**: Data Breach
  - **Example**: Exposure of client personal information
  - **Classification**: Compliance, Notification Required

## Retail
- **Incident Type**: Theft
  - **Example**: Shoplifting incident
  - **Classification**: Loss Prevention, Police Notification Possible

- **Incident Type**: Supply Chain Disruption
  - **Example**: Supplier bankruptcy
  - **Classification**: Operational, Emergency Sourcing Required

## Utilities
- **Incident Type**: Power Outage
  - **Example**: Storm-related outage
  - **Classification**: Emergency, Response Required

- **Incident Type**: Infrastructure Failure
  - **Example**: Water main break
  - **Classification**: Public Safety, Immediate Action Required

## Construction
- **Incident Type**: Site Accident
  - **Example**: Fall from height
  - **Classification**: Safety Incident, Report Required

- **Incident Type**: Regulatory Violation
  - **Example**: Building without a permit
  - **Classification**: Compliance, Legal Action Possible

## Corporate
- **Incident Type**: Internal Fraud
  - **Example**: Embezzlement
  - **Classification**: Legal, Investigation Required

- **Incident Type**: Data Loss
  - **Example**: Server crash resulting in lost data
  - **Classification**: Operational, Recovery Plan Required

## Technology
- **Incident Type**: System Outage
  - **Example**: Website down due to server failure
  - **Classification**: Technical Incident, Immediate Resolution Required

- **Incident Type**: Security Breach
  - **Example**: Unauthorized access to software environment
  - **Classification**: Threat Response, Investigation Required

## Education
- **Incident Type**: Student Incident
  - **Example**: Bullying or harassment complaint
  - **Classification**: Safety and Welfare, Immediate Action Required

- **Incident Type**: Academic Fraud
  - **Example**: Plagiarism in a thesis
  - **Classification**: Academic Integrity, Review Required

## Conclusion
Understanding the various types of incidents across different industries is crucial for effective response and management. This documentation should serve as a guide for identifying, classifying, and responding to incidents appropriately.
